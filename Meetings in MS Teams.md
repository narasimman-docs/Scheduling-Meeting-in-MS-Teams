## Overview

A Microsoft Teams meeting is an online collaboration session that lets participants communicate with audio, video, screen sharing, chat, file sharing, and collaborative applications. A scheduled meeting creates a calendar event, sends invitations to participants, and reserves a meeting space that attendees can join at the scheduled time.

When you connect Microsoft Teams to Microsoft Outlook, meeting information syncs automatically between both applications. This integration helps keep the meeting details consistent across both apps and makes it easier for organizers and attendees to manage the event.

---

## Benefits

Microsoft Teams meetings provide the following benefits:

- Schedule meetings in advance.
- Invite internal and external participants.
- Create recurring meetings.
- View participant availability with Scheduling Assistant.
- Join meetings from desktop, web, or mobile devices.
- Share files before, during, and after meetings.
- Maintain meeting chat before and after the meeting.
- Support webinars and town halls when enabled by the organization.

# Schedule a Meeting in Microsoft Teams

Schedule a Microsoft Teams meeting and send invitations to participants.

---

# Method 1 — Schedule from the Teams Calendar

## Prerequisites

- Sign in to Microsoft Teams.
- Have permission to create meetings.
- Know the participant names or email addresses.
- Verify that the **Calendar** app is available.

## Procedure

1. From your desktop, open **Microsoft Teams**.
2. In the left navigation pane, select **Calendar**.
3. In the **Calendar** view, select **New Meeting**.
4. In the **Add title** field, enter the meeting title.
5. In the **Enter name or email** field, enter the participant's name or email address. Then select the participant from the suggestions.
6. (Optional) In the **Enter name or email** field, add optional attendees.
7. In the **Duration** field, select the following:
   - Meeting start date and time
   - Meeting end date and time.
8. (Optional) In the **Repeat** field, select a recurrence pattern.
9. (Optional) In the **Advanced options** field, configure **Meeting access**, **Roles**, or **Participation**.
> **Note**: **Advanced options** are available only for accounts with a supported Microsoft 365 plan.
10. (Optional) In the **Add location** field, enter the meeting location for a hybrid meeting.
11. (Optional) In the meeting details area, enter the meeting description.
12. Review the meeting details.
13. Select **Send**.

Teams creates the meeting and sends invitations to participants.

# Method 2 — Schedule from Chat

Schedule a meeting from an existing chat.

## Prerequisites

- Sign in to Microsoft Teams.
- Have an existing one-to-one or group chat.
- Have permission to create meetings.

## Procedure

1. From your desktop, open **Microsoft Teams**.
2. In the left navigation pane, select **Chat**.
3. Open the required one-to-one or group conversation.
4. In the upper-right corner of the conversation, select **More options**.
5. Select **Schedule a meeting**.
6. In the **Add title** field, enter the meeting title.
7. In the **Enter name or email** field, add or update the participant list, if required.
8. In the **Duration** field, select the following:
   - Meeting start date and time
   - Meeting end date and time
9. (Optional) In the **Repeat** field, select a recurrence pattern.
10. (Optional) In the **Advanced options** field, configure **Meeting access**, **Roles**, or **Participation**.

    > **Note**: **Advanced options** are available only for accounts with a supported Microsoft 365 plan.
11. (Optional) In the **Add location** field, enter the meeting location for a hybrid meeting.
12. (Optional) In the meeting details area, enter the meeting description.
13. Review the meeting details and attendee list.
14. Select **Send**.

Teams creates the meeting and sends invitations to participants.

# Method 3 — Schedule a Channel Meeting

Schedule a meeting for a Teams channel.

## Prerequisites

- Sign in to Microsoft Teams.
- Be a member of the target team.
- Have permission to create channel meetings.

### Procedure

1. From your desktop, open **Microsoft Teams**.
2. In the left navigation pane, select **Teams**.
3. Select the required team.
4. Select the required channel.
5. In the upper-right corner, select the arrow next to **Meet now**, and then select **Schedule a meeting**.
6. In the **Add title** field, enter the meeting title.
7. In the **Enter name or email** field, add attendees, if required.
8. In the **Duration** field, select the following:

   * Meeting start date and time
   * Meeting end date and time
9. (Optional) In the **Repeat** field, select a recurrence pattern.
10. (Optional) In the **Advanced options** field, configure **Meeting access**, **Roles**, or **Participation**.

    > **Note**: **Advanced options** are available only for accounts with a supported Microsoft 365 plan.
11. (Optional) In the **Add location** field, enter the meeting location for a hybrid meeting.
12. (Optional) In the meeting details area, enter the meeting description.
13. Review the meeting details and attendee list.
14. Select **Send**.

The meeting appears in the selected channel.

# Method 4 — Schedule from Microsoft Outlook

Create a Teams meeting from Outlook.

## Prerequisites

- Sign in to Microsoft Outlook.
- Ensure Outlook is connected to Microsoft 365.
- Ensure the Microsoft Teams add-in is enabled.

### Procedure

1. From your desktop, open **Microsoft Outlook**.
2. In the **Home** tab, select **New Event**.
3. Select **Teams Meeting**.
4. In the **Add a title** field, enter the meeting title.
5. In the **Invite attendees** field, enter the participant's name or email address, and then select the participant from the suggestions.
6. (Optional) In the **Invite attendees** field, add optional attendees.
7. In the **Date and time** section, select the following:

   * Meeting start date and time
   * Meeting end date and time
8. (Optional) In the **Repeat** field, select a recurrence pattern.
9. (Optional) In the **Advanced options** field, configure **Meeting access**, **Roles**, or **Participation**.

   > **Note**: **Advanced options** are available only for accounts with a supported Microsoft 365 plan.
10. (Optional) In the **Location** field, enter the meeting location for a hybrid meeting.
11. (Optional) In the meeting details area, enter the meeting description.
12. Review the meeting details and attendee list.
13. Select **Send**.

Teams synchronizes the meeting with Microsoft Outlook.

> **Tip**
> Use **Scheduling Assistant** to identify available meeting times before sending invitations.

## Meeting scheduling fields

Use the following table to understand the fields you can configure when you create or edit a meeting. Optional fields are marked as optional.

| Field | Required | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| Title | Yes | The meeting title that appears in calendars. |
| Required attendees | Yes | Participants who should attend. |
| Optional attendees | No | Additional participants. |
| Date | Yes | The meeting date. |
| Start time | Yes | The meeting start time. |
| End time | Yes | The meeting end time. |
| Time zone | No | The meeting time zone. |
| Location | No | The physical meeting location. |
| Description | No | The meeting agenda or notes. |
| Recurrence | No | The repeat schedule. |
| Channel | No | The associated Teams channel. |

## Meeting options

Use the following table to review meeting options. These options control attendee access and meeting behavior.

| Option | Description |
|---------|-------------|

| **Camera** | Controls attendee camera access. |
| **Meeting chat** | Controls chat availability. |
| **Recording** | Allows recording when recording is permitted. |
